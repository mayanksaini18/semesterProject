Overview
The Psychology of Money explores the emotional and psychological aspects of money, wealth, and financial decision-making. It emphasizes the importance of behavior over intelligence when it comes to financial success.

Lessons

Wealth is not about income; it’s about saving and investing.
Compounding is the most powerful force in finance.
Risk and luck play a bigger role in success than most people realize.
Financial independence is more valuable than luxury purchases.
The hardest skill in finance is controlling emotions and sticking to a plan.

About the Author

Morgan Housel is an award-winning financial writer, investor, and partner at The Collaborative Fund. He has written for The Wall Street Journal and The Motley Fool, offering unique insights into money and human behavior.
